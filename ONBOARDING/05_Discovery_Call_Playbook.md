# Discovery Call Playbook
### Appointment Type 1 of 2

---

## Purpose

The Discovery Call exists to gather needs, timeline, and basics — nothing more. **You do not quote pricing on this call.** That happens after Marcel sets pricing and pitch direction from your handoff summary. Trying to close on discovery is the single most common way this process breaks — see `19_Failure_Modes_and_Corrections.md`.

---

## The Discovery Call Agenda (20–30 min)

| Segment | Time | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Rapport + Agenda | 2 min | Set expectations for the call so nobody's guessing what happens next |
| 2. LEAK Deep-Dive | 10–12 min | Go past your phone-qualifying and get the real detail on Look/Experience/Appear/Keep-up |
| 3. Timeline + Decision Process | 5 min | Who else needs to weigh in, what's driving urgency (or not), rough budget context |
| 4. Recap + Set Next-Step Expectations | 3 min | Confirm what happens next and when |

### Opening the Call

```
"Thanks for grabbing time, [NAME]. Here's the plan for the next 20 minutes: I want to
understand where things stand with your website and how you're currently getting new
[patients/clients/calls], and then I'll take everything back to our team so we can put
together the right recommendation — sound good?"
```

That last line matters: it tells the prospect, up front, that pricing is coming *later*, from *the team* — not from you, not today. This sets up the handoff cleanly and prevents an awkward "so what does this cost" moment you're not ready to answer.

## LEAK Discovery Question Bank

Pick the questions that fit the conversation — you don't need all of these every time.

**L — Look**
- "When's the last time the site was redesigned or refreshed?"
- "If a new [patient/client] pulls up the site on their phone right now, what's their first impression, honestly?"

**E — Experience**
- "Walk me through what happens if someone tries to contact you from the site on their phone — how easy is that?"
- "Do you know if people are dropping off before filling out a form or making a call?"

**A — Appear**
- "Where do you show up when someone searches [service] + [city] — have you checked recently?"
- "How's your Google Business Profile looking — reviews, response rate, all that?"

**K — Keep-up**
- "Roughly how many new [patients/clients/calls] come from the website or Google each month right now?"
- "Is that enough, or are you still leaning heavily on referrals/word of mouth to fill the gap?"

**Timeline & Decision Process**
- "Is this something you're looking to move on soon, or more of a 'when it's right' thing?"
- "Besides you, is anyone else involved in a decision like this?"
- "Have you worked with an agency before on this kind of thing? How'd that go?"

---

## What NOT To Do On This Call

- ❌ Don't quote a price, even a range, even if pushed. Redirect: *"I want to make sure whatever we recommend actually fits — let me get this in front of our team first so the number is real, not a guess."*
- ❌ Don't promise specific results (guaranteed lead counts, "you'll rank #1," case outcomes for law firms). See niche playbooks for compliance-sensitive language, especially law and behavioral health.
- ❌ Don't let the call run long without an agenda — busy owners disengage fast if it feels aimless.
- ❌ Don't skip the decision-process question. Finding out post-handoff that you were talking to someone who can't actually say yes wastes everyone's time.
