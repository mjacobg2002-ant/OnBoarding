# Failure Modes + Corrections
### What reps do wrong, and how to fix it — read this before going live, and revisit any time performance dips.

| # | Failure Mode | Why It Happens | Correction | Drill To Fix It |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pitching price before qualifying | Nerves, or trying to move fast | Always complete LEAK qualifying before any pricing talk — and remember, Marcel sets pricing, not the rep | Re-run Basic roleplays, score strictly on qualifying-before-pitching |
| 2 | Talking more than listening on discovery calls | Trying to "sell" instead of "discover" | Target ≤40% talk time on discovery; ask open-ended questions and let silence sit | Record a discovery roleplay, review talk-time split afterward |
| 3 | Weak or missing appointment confirmation (no specific day/time) | Avoiding the direct ask, fear of rejection | Always confirm an exact date/time, send a calendar invite immediately, confirm again via SMS/email | Drill Step 6 of the 6-Step Cold Call Flow in isolation, 10 reps in a row |
| 4 | Vague Marcel Handoff Summaries ("they seem interested, need pricing") | Rushing after a call, not using the template | Use the structured Handoff Summary template every time, no exceptions, no freeform paragraphs | Practice writing 2 handoffs from roleplay notes before going live |
| 5 | Skipping decision-maker confirmation | Assuming whoever answers is the right person | Confirm role early in the call — don't invest a full discovery call in the wrong person | Add a decision-maker-role question to every Basic roleplay warm-up |
| 6 | Over-promising results or guarantees | Trying to close harder, especially under price pressure | Never guarantee specific lead counts or case outcomes — speak in visibility/process terms only, especially in law and behavioral health | Review niche playbook compliance-notes sections; roleplay the specific "how much does this cost" and "what if it doesn't work" objections |
| 7 | Not logging HubSpot activity same-day | Batching admin to "later," which never comes | Log immediately after each call/appointment — build it into the time-block, not an afterthought | Add a hard stop at the end of every Prime Dial Block to log before moving on |
| 8 | Ghost-booking appointments to hit quota | Pressure to hit appointment targets | Enforce a LEAK-score minimum (7+) before booking — quality over quantity, always | Manager spot-checks a sample of booked appointments weekly against LEAK scores logged |
| 9 | No structured follow-up after "no answer" | Losing track of where a lead is in the cadence | Follow the Multi-Touch Cadence in `11_Outreach_Templates_Multichannel.md` fully before marking a lead dead | Weekly HubSpot audit: any lead with 0 touches in 3+ days gets flagged |
| 10 | Freezing or getting defensive on objections | Objection handling isn't automatic yet | Drill P.V.R.A. daily via the 60-Second Objection Rep drill in `06_Objection_Handling_Master.md` | 10 rounds minimum, every day, until it's reflexive |

---

## How Managers Should Use This File

When a rep is underperforming (see `14_30Day_Ramp_Plan.md`, "What To Do If a Rep Is Behind Target"), don't guess at the cause — pull 2–3 recent calls, run them against `20_Call_QA_Checklist.md`, and match what you see to a specific row above. Fix the specific pattern with the specific drill, rather than a general "try harder" conversation.
