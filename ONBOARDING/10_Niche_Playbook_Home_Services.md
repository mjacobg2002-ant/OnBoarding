# Niche Playbook: HVAC + Home Services

Covers: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, restoration, pest control.

---

## 1. Niche Snapshot

Home services owners are typically hands-on operators — often out on jobs, not sitting at a desk. They think in practical, ROI terms: calls booked, jobs closed, trucks rolling. They respond well to directness and numbers, less well to abstract "branding" language. Urgency and seasonality matter a lot here (AC failures in summer, heating in winter, storm damage for roofing/restoration) — timing your outreach around that is a real edge.

---

## 2. Why They Need Us (LEAK Mapped to This Niche)

| LEAK Letter | Common Pattern in Home Services |
|---|---|
| **L — Look** | Site looks dated or overly generic/templated, doesn't stand out from every other local contractor site |
| **E — Experience** | No click-to-call prominent on mobile, slow load times, forms that don't work well for someone standing in front of a broken unit |
| **A — Appear** | Getting outranked by larger regional/national chains and heavily-advertised competitors on Google Maps and Local Services Ads |
| **K — Keep-up** | Inbound call volume inconsistent, heavy reliance on repeat customers/word of mouth, limited capacity to handle seasonal demand spikes |

---

## 3. Decision-Maker Map

| Title | Notes |
|---|---|
| Owner | Most common decision-maker, especially single-location businesses |
| GM | Common at larger or multi-location operations; may still need owner sign-off on spend |

**Reaching them:** owners are often on job sites — early morning (before dispatch) or end of day tends to work better than mid-day. Expect to leave more voicemails here than in other niches; the multi-touch cadence matters more.

---

## 4. Tone & Compliance Notes

- Speak in terms of call volume, booked jobs, and visibility — not vague "branding" language. This audience wants to know what's actually going to change.
- Be aware of seasonality — leading with "it's about to be your busy season, is your site ready to handle the calls?" lands better than a generic pitch in the wrong month.
- Don't overpromise specific lead-volume numbers without Marcel's sign-off — same principle as every other niche.

## 5. Cold Call Script (Niche Version)

```
STEP 1 — OPENER
"Hey [Name], this is [Rep] — I know this is a cold call, got 30 seconds?"

STEP 2 — REASON FOR CALL
"I was looking at [Company Name]'s Google listing and had a quick question about
how calls are coming in right now — got a second?"

STEP 3 — PERMISSION
"Mind if I ask a couple quick questions?"

STEP 4 — LEAK DISCOVERY
"When's the last time the website was actually updated?"
"If someone searches '[service] near me' right now, does [Company Name] show up,
or mostly the bigger competitors?"
"Roughly how many calls are coming in from the website or Google each month
versus repeat/referral customers?"

STEP 5 — VALUE BRIDGE
"That's really common right now — [season/urgency angle if relevant, e.g., 'with
summer coming, AC call volume is about to spike'] — a lot of solid companies are
losing calls to competitors who just show up higher on Google, not because
they're better."

STEP 6 — ASK FOR APPOINTMENT
"Worth 20 minutes to look at this properly? I've got [time] or [time], which's
easier?"
```

## 10. Common Objections (Niche-Specific) — P.V.R.A.

### "We're slammed already, don't need more calls right now"
> **V:** "Good problem to have, honestly."
> **R:** "A lot of companies still want to build this out now so it's ready before the next slow stretch — the site work and ranking take time to kick in anyway."
> **A:** "Worth a quick look now so it's working for you by the time things quiet down?"

### "What makes you different from the last 5 agencies that called me?"
> **V:** "Fair — you've probably heard a lot of the same pitch."
> **R:** "Honestly the difference isn't a claim I can make over the phone — it's whether the recommendation actually fits your specific business, which is why I want to actually look at your situation instead of pitching something generic."
> **A:** "Worth 15 minutes to see if it's actually relevant, no obligation either way?"

### "We have an internal person/team handling marketing already"
> **V:** "Good to have that in-house."
> **R:** "Totally fine — sometimes it's useful to get an outside look at specific things like the website or Google ranking as a second opinion, separate from whatever they're focused on."
> **A:** "Worth a quick, no-pressure look just to compare notes?"

## 11. Good vs. Bad Example

**Scenario:** Owner says "just send me some info, I'm mid-job."

| ❌ Bad Rep | ✅ Good Rep |
|---|---|
| "Sure, what's your email?" (gives up, no attempt to lock a real time) | "No problem — I'll send something over either way. When's a better time to actually grab 10 minutes, morning or end of day?" |
