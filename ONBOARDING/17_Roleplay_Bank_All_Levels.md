# Roleplay Bank — All Levels

Trainer plays the prospect. Rep runs the call live, no notes for Intermediate/Advanced tiers. Score every roleplay against `18_Evaluation_Rubrics_and_Certification.md`.

---

## BASIC TIER
*Receptive-ish prospect, one soft objection. Goal: clean execution of the 6-Step Cold Call Flow.*

### BH-Basic-1 — Sunrise Recovery Center

**Setup:** Trainer plays Dana, owner/founder of a small IOP program. Busy but not hostile.

> **Trainer says:** "Hello, this is Dana."
> **Rep says:** "Hey Dana, this is [Rep] — I know this is a cold call, got 30 seconds?"
> **Trainer says:** "Uh, sure, I guess."
> **Rep says:** "I was actually looking at Sunrise Recovery Center's website and had a quick question about how new clients are finding you — got a second?"
> **Trainer says:** "I mean, I guess so, but I really don't have much time to talk about this right now."
> **Rep says (aim for):** "Totally fair, I'll be quick — just one question: is most of your intake coming from the website and Google, or mostly referrals?"
> **Trainer says:** "Mostly referrals, honestly."
> **Rep says (aim for):** [Value bridge + ask for appointment] "That's really common — a lot of great programs are getting buried under the big referral marketplaces online even though the program itself is stronger. Worth 20 minutes to actually look at this? I've got Tuesday at 10 or Wednesday at 2."
> **Trainer says:** "Wednesday at 2 works."

**Grade against:** Cold Call Rubric.

## INTERMEDIATE TIER
*Busier prospect, two stacked objections, possible gatekeeper. Goal: P.V.R.A. discipline under mild pressure.*

### BH-Intermediate-1 — Cedar Ridge Therapy Group

**Setup:** Front desk screens first, then owner. Owner brings up a bad past agency experience and price.

> **Trainer says (as front desk):** "Cedar Ridge, this is Amy."
> **Rep says:** [Direct, brief reason for calling, asks for owner]
> **Trainer says:** "She's with a client right now, can I ask what this is regarding?"
> **Rep says (aim for):** "It's a quick business question about the website, for her directly — what's a good time to try back?"
> **Trainer says (as owner, later):** "This is Jamie."
> **Rep says:** [Opener + reason for call + LEAK question]
> **Trainer says:** "Honestly we tried a marketing company before and it was a waste of money."
> **Rep says (aim for):** "That's frustrating, and pretty common, unfortunately. Can I ask what specifically didn't work — communication, results, or just not built for a group like yours?"
> **Trainer says:** "Mostly just no results, and it was expensive too. How much does this cost anyway?"
> **Rep says (aim for):** "Fair question — honestly it depends on what you actually need, which is why I'd rather ask a couple questions first than throw out a number that doesn't fit. Can I ask two quick things?"

**Grade against:** Cold Call Rubric + Objection Handling Rubric.

### LAW-Intermediate-1 — Alvarez Personal Injury

**Setup:** Trainer plays a partner, short on time, raises a reputational concern and a decision-process objection.

> **Trainer says:** "This is Maria, make it quick."

> **Rep says (aim for):** "Makes sense — would a short summary you could forward help, or would a quick call with both of you work better?"

**Grade against:** Cold Call Rubric + Objection Handling Rubric.

### HS-Intermediate-1 — Coastal Air HVAC

**Setup:** Trainer plays the owner, mid-job, raises a seasonal-capacity objection and a "why you" objection.

## ADVANCED TIER
*Hostile, rushed, or sophisticated prospect. Stacked objections + a curveball. Goal: composure and P.V.R.A. under real pressure.*

### BH-Advanced-1 — Overlook Behavioral Health

**Setup:** Trainer plays a Clinical Director — skeptical, rushed, brings up compliance and a recent bad vendor experience.

> **Trainer says:** "This is Dr. Reyes, I have about 90 seconds."
> **Rep says:** [Fast, sharp opener + one LEAK question, no wasted words]
> **Trainer says:** "We actually just signed with another marketing company two months ago and it's not working. Why would you be any different? And how do you even handle HIPAA on your end?"
> **Rep says (aim for):** "Fair on both counts. On the compliance side — we build every form and workflow with privacy top of mind, happy to walk through specifics if we talk further. On the other agency — can I ask what specifically isn't working? Sometimes it's the plan, sometimes it's just too early to tell."
> **Trainer says:** "It's just not generating calls. I really don't have more time today."
> **Rep says (aim for):** "Totally get it — can I just grab 15 minutes later this week to actually look at what's happening, no commitment either way?"

**Grade against:** Full rubric set (Cold Call + Objection Handling). This scenario doubles as a certification-tier evaluation.

### LAW-Advanced-1 — Solo Practitioner, Attorney Dominic Reyes

**Setup:** Trainer plays an irritated solo attorney, tries to end the call quickly twice.

> **Trainer says:** "Yeah? How'd you even get this number?"
> **Rep says (aim for):** "Public listing — I'll be quick. Got 20 seconds?"
> **Trainer says:** "I really don't do this digital marketing stuff, everything's referral-based, I'm hanging up."
> **Rep says (aim for):** "Totally fair — one thing before you go: if referrals ever slowed down for a stretch, is there a backup, or would that actually hurt?"
> **Trainer says:** "...It would hurt, yeah, but I don't have time for this right now."
> **Rep says (aim for):** "Understood — can I just send a two-line email so it's easy to reconnect if that ever changes, no calls involved?"

**Grade against:** Full rubric set. Success here isn't necessarily a booked appointment — a clean, professional exit with a door left open is an acceptable outcome and should be scored as such.

### General-Advanced-1 — Certification Final Exam

**Setup:** Trainer picks any niche and any fictional business. No script given to the rep in advance. Trainer stacks three objections back-to-back from the Universal Objection Bank (`06_Objection_Handling_Master.md`) plus one niche-specific objection, with minimal pausing between them, then either agrees to a time or ends the call.

**Purpose:** This is the final gate before certification (see `18_Evaluation_Rubrics_and_Certification.md`). It is designed to feel a little chaotic on purpose — real calls sometimes are. Composure, not perfection, is what's being measured.
