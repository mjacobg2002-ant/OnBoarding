# ICP + Qualifying Framework

---

## 1. Who We're Looking For (Ideal Customer Profile)

Across all three niches, the pattern is the same: **independent or small-group local/regional operators** — not national chains, not solo freelancers with no real business — where the owner or a small leadership team makes the buying decision directly, and the current website and Google presence are clearly behind where the business itself actually is.

| Niche | Typical Business | Typical Decision-Maker |
|---|---|---|
| Behavioral Health | Independent treatment center, assisted living facility, single or multi-location therapy group | Owner, Clinical Director, Practice Owner |
| Law Firms | Solo or small-partner firm, boutique practice | Partner, Managing Attorney, Owner |
| Home Services | Independent or regional HVAC/plumbing/electrical/roofing/restoration/pest company | Owner, GM |

**Rule of thumb:** if the person on the phone can say "yes, let's do it" without checking with three other people, you're probably talking to the right person or one call away from them.

## 2. The LEAK Score

Every qualifying conversation is really answering one question: *is this business leaking potential revenue because of how they show up online?* LEAK is how you check, fast, on a live call.

| Letter | Stands For | What You're Listening/Looking For |
|---|---|---|
| **L** | **Look** | Does the website look outdated, generic, or unprofessional relative to the quality of the business itself? |
| **E** | **Experience** | Is the mobile experience broken, slow, or hard to act on (no clear call button, clunky forms, doesn't load well)? |
| **A** | **Appear** | Does the business show up on page 1 of Google/Maps for obvious searches in their area? Is their Google Business Profile thin, unclaimed, or under-reviewed? |
| **K** | **Keep-up** | Is inbound call/lead volume too low for what the business needs — are they relying almost entirely on referrals or word of mouth? |

### Scoring

Score each letter **0–3** during or immediately after the call:

- **0** — No real issue here
- **1** — Minor issue
- **2** — Moderate issue, clearly costing them something
- **3** — Severe issue, obviously costing them business

**Total: /12**

| Score | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–3 | Not qualified right now | Log in HubSpot, move to long-term nurture, don't book an appointment |
| 4–6 | Marginal | Dig one layer deeper before booking — confirm there's real pain, not just a mild annoyance |
| 7–9 | Qualified | Book the Discovery appointment |
| 10–12 | Priority | Book ASAP and flag to Marcel as high-value in your handoff notes |

## 3. Pre-Call Research Checklist (Do This Before You Dial)

You should never cold-call a business you know nothing about. Two to three minutes of research per lead makes every call sharper.

- [ ] Pull up their Google Business Profile — note review count, review recency, star rating, and whether it's claimed/optimized
- [ ] Search their core service + city (e.g., "IOP program [city]," "personal injury lawyer [city]," "HVAC repair [city]") — note where they rank, if at all
- [ ] Open their website on a phone-width browser window — note load speed and mobile usability
- [ ] Check 1–2 competitors in the same search results — note what "good" looks like in their specific market
- [ ] Identify the likely decision-maker by name if possible (About page, LinkedIn, state licensing directories for law/BH)

This is the raw material for your LEAK score before the prospect ever picks up.

## 4. Where Leads Come From

| Source | Best For |
| --- | --- |
| Google Maps | Fast volume, geographic targeting, visible review/rating data at a glance |
| Google Search | Confirms who is (and isn't) ranking — the businesses *not* showing up are often your best targets |
| Directories (industry-specific: e.g., attorney directories, treatment locators, HomeAdvisor-style listings) | Niche-dense lists, often with more contact detail |
| Competitor research | If a competitor is dominating a market, the businesses losing to them are motivated |
