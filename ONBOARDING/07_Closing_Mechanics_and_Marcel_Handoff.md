# Closing Mechanics + Marcel Handoff
### Appointment Type 2 of 2, and everything in between

---

## The Full Sequence (Reference)

```
Discovery Call  →  Handoff Summary to Marcel  →  Marcel sets pricing + pitch direction
     →  Close/Presentation Call  →  Contract + Deposit (Stripe)  →  Retainer begins at signature
```

Every deal moves through this exact sequence. No skipping steps, no quoting pricing early, no closing without Marcel's direction.

---

## Step 1: The Marcel Handoff Summary

Send this **immediately** after every Discovery Call — same day, ideally within the hour while it's fresh. A slow or vague handoff is the #1 way good discovery calls turn into dead deals. See `19_Failure_Modes_and_Corrections.md`.

```
SUBJECT: Discovery Summary — [Business Name] — [Niche]

Business Name: ______________________
Niche: [Behavioral Health / Law / Home Services]

  A: ___   Notes: ______________________
  K: ___   Notes: ______________________

TIMELINE / URGENCY: ______________________

DECISION PROCESS: ______________________

BUDGET SIGNALS (if mentioned): ______________________

CURRENT MARKETING SITUATION: ______________________

AD SPEND INTEREST: [Yes / No / Unsure]

REP'S RECOMMENDED DIRECTION: [Starter / Standard / Premium website]
                              [Care / Growth / Premium Growth retainer]
                              (Rep's gut read only — Marcel confirms final direction)

```

**Rule:** this is not a paragraph of prose — it's a structured form, every time, same fields, same order. Marcel should be able to scan it in 30 seconds. This also mirrors the HubSpot deal properties in `16_HubSpot_Pipeline_and_Lead_Tracking.md` — log the same information there, not just in a message to Marcel.

## Step 2: Marcel Sets Pricing + Pitch Direction

This is Marcel's step, not the rep's. The rep waits for direction before booking the close call framing (though the close call itself can often be scheduled provisionally at the end of discovery — see below).

> **🔧 Confirm with Marcel:** expected turnaround time between handoff and pricing direction, so reps can set an accurate expectation with the prospect (e.g., "our team will have a recommendation put together within X business days").

**Best practice:** at the end of the Discovery Call, provisionally pencil in a Close Call time ("let's tentatively grab time [X days out] to go over what we put together") so you're not starting a second scheduling negotiation cold. Confirm the exact time once Marcel's direction is in hand.

---

## Step 3: The Close/Presentation Call

| Segment | Goal |
|---|---|
| 1. Recap Discovery | Remind them what they told you — shows you listened, re-establishes the pain |
| 2. Present the Recommended Solution | As directed by Marcel — the specific tier/package, framed around their stated pain, not a generic pitch |
| 3. Present the Investment | The price, framed clearly and confidently — no apologizing for the number |
| 4. Handle Objections | P.V.R.A., every time — see `06_Objection_Handling_Master.md` |
| 5. Ask For The Close | Direct ask — "Let's get this moving — I can send the agreement over right now" |
| 6. Collect Deposit + Signature | Stripe link/invoice, contract signature |

### Sample Framing Language

```
"So last time we talked, you mentioned [RECAP THEIR STATED PAIN]. Based on that, here's
what our team put together for you: [PRESENT RECOMMENDED TIER/PACKAGE]. The investment
for that is [PRICE]. Want me to go ahead and send the agreement over so we can get
started?"
```

Notice: state the price plainly, then ask directly. Don't trail off after the number waiting for a reaction — that's where hesitation creeps in.

---

## Step 4: Contract + Deposit (Stripe)

- [ ] Send contract for signature
- [ ] Send Stripe deposit link/invoice
- [ ] Confirm both are completed before treating the deal as Closed Won
- [ ] Retainer work begins on the **signature date**, not the discovery date or the handoff date

> **🔧 Confirm with Marcel:** exact deposit amount/percentage and the specific Stripe workflow (invoice vs. payment link, who sends it).

---

## Step 5: Immediately After Close

- [ ] Log the deal as **Closed Won** in HubSpot with final tier/price
- [ ] Notify Marcel that contract + deposit are both confirmed
- [ ] Hand off to whoever owns fulfillment/delivery at KSD

> **🔧 Confirm with Marcel:** who owns fulfillment after a deal closes, so this handoff has a clear owner rather than assuming the rep continues managing delivery.

---

## Good vs. Bad: Presenting the Investment

| ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
|---|---|
| "So, um, the price is kind of, it comes out to around $8,000, but we can talk about that..." (apologetic, vague) | "The investment for the Standard package is $8,000. I can send the agreement over right now so we can get moving." |
| Presenting price then going silent and anxious, or immediately justifying before they've reacted | Presenting price, then directly asking for the close, and letting silence do its job |
