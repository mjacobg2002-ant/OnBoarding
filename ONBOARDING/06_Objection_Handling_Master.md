# Objection Handling Master
### The P.V.R.A. Framework

---

## The Framework

Every objection, on every call, gets handled the same four-step way:

| Step | What It Means | What It Sounds Like |
|---|---|---|
| **P — Pause** | Don't jump in defensively. Let them finish. Take a breath. | (silence, then a calm tone) |
| **V — Validate** | Acknowledge the objection is reasonable — don't argue with it. | "Totally fair," "Makes sense," "I'd probably say the same thing" |
| **R — Reframe** | Shift the frame back to their actual goal or pain, not your pitch. | "That's actually exactly why I called, not a reason not to..." |
| **A — Advance** | Ask a question or propose a small next step — don't launch into a full pitch. | "Can I ask you one thing?" / "What if we just looked at..." |

**The most common mistake:** skipping straight to Reframe/Advance without Pause/Validate. It makes the rep sound like they're steamrolling the objection instead of hearing it. Slow down.

## Universal Objection Bank

Niche-specific objections and responses live in files 08–10. These are the ones you'll hear regardless of niche.

### "I'm not interested"
> **V:** "Totally get it — you weren't expecting this call."
> **R:** "Can I ask — is it that the website/marketing side is already handled, or just that cold calls aren't your thing?"
> **A:** (Branch based on answer — if "already handled," ask one LEAK question; if "just don't like cold calls," acknowledge and ask permission for one quick question anyway.)

### "Just send me some info"
> **V:** "Happy to — that's easy."
> **R:** "I'll send something over, but honestly the more useful thing is 10 minutes live so it's actually relevant to your business instead of a generic PDF."
> **A:** "Can I grab 10 minutes [day/time] instead — and I'll still send a note either way?"

### "We don't have budget for this right now"
> **V:** "Makes sense, budget's real."
> **R:** "A lot of the businesses we work with felt the same way going in — this usually isn't an add-on cost, it's meant to pay for itself in new business. Worth at least seeing what that could look like for you specifically?"
> **A:** "Let's do 15 minutes so at least you've got real numbers to decide with, no pressure."

### "Call me back later / bad timing"
> **V:** "No problem at all."
> **R:** (none needed — just confirm)
> **A:** "When's genuinely better — later today, or should I just grab a real day/time now so it doesn't fall through the cracks?" (Always try to lock a specific time before hanging up.)

### "We already have someone/an agency for this"
> **V:** "Good — glad that's covered."
> **R:** "Out of curiosity, how's that going — are you seeing the results you'd want from it?"
> **A:** (If lukewarm: "Would it be worth a quick second opinion, no pressure to switch anything?" If genuinely happy: thank them, log as nurture, exit gracefully.)

### "How much does this cost?" (asked too early, before discovery)
> **V:** "Fair question."
> **R:** "It really depends on what you actually need — which is exactly what I want to figure out first so I'm not just throwing a number at you that doesn't fit."
> **A:** "Can I ask two quick questions so any number I eventually give you is actually real?"

### "I need to think about it" (on a close call)
> **V:** "Of course — this shouldn't be a rushed decision."
> **R:** "Can I ask what specifically you want to think through — is it the investment, the timing, or something about the plan itself?"
> **A:** (Branch based on the real concern — often this collapses into one of the other objections above.)

### "We tried an agency before and it didn't work"
> **V:** "That's frustrating, and honestly common — a lot of agencies overpromise."
> **R:** "Can I ask what specifically didn't work — was it communication, results, or just not the right fit for your niche?"
> **A:** "That's useful to know — makes sense why you'd be cautious. Mind if I ask a couple questions about where things stand today?"

### "I'm not the right person for this"
> **V:** "No worries — appreciate you saying so."
> **R:** (none needed)
> **A:** "Who would be the best person to talk to, and is there a good way to get introduced?"

### "I'm slammed, don't have time to talk"
> **V:** "Totally understand — I'll be quick."
> **R:** (skip straight to one sharp question)
> **A:** "One question, 10 seconds: are you happy with how many new [patients/clients/calls] are coming from the website right now?" (Then either book a real time or let them go.)

## Good vs. Bad: Handling "Just Send Me Info"

| ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
|---|---|
| "Okay sure, what's your email?" (gives up immediately, no attempt to advance) | "Happy to — but 10 minutes live would actually be more useful than a generic PDF. Does [day/time] work, and I'll still follow up with a note either way?" |
| Arguing: "Info won't really tell you anything though..." (sounds defensive) | Validating first, then reframing toward a specific next step |

---

## Practice Drill: 60-Second Objection Reps

**Setup:** Pair up (or trainer + rep). One person calls out an objection at random from this bank. The other has 5 seconds to start their P.V.R.A. response out loud, no notes. Rotate every 60 seconds. Do 10 rounds minimum per practice session.

**Goal:** the P.V.R.A. shape should become automatic — Pause/Validate should never be skipped, even under pressure.
