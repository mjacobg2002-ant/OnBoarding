# Outreach Templates — Multichannel + Cadence
### Generic, adaptable versions. Niche-flavored versions live in files 08–10.

---

## The Multi-Touch Cadence

Don't dial once and give up, and don't hammer a lead daily forever either. Follow this rhythm, then move stale leads to long-term nurture instead of daily-dialing dead ends:

| Day | Touch |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Call 1 (AM) + Email 1 same day if no answer |
| Day 2 | SMS 1 |
| Day 3 | Call 2 (different time of day than Call 1) |
| Day 5 | LinkedIn connection request + short note |
| Day 7 | Call 3 + Email 2 (value-add angle) |
| Day 10 | Call 4 + Breakup Email |
| Day 10+ | No response → move to long-term nurture (quarterly check-in), stop daily touches |

Log every touch in HubSpot the same day it happens — see `16_HubSpot_Pipeline_and_Lead_Tracking.md`.

## Generic Email Templates

**Initial:**
```
SUBJECT: Quick question about [Business Name]

Hi [Name],

I was looking at [Business Name] and had a quick question about how new
[patients/clients/calls] are finding you right now — mostly the website/Google,
or more word of mouth/referral?

Worth 15 minutes to see where things stand and what's realistic to improve?

[Rep Name]
Kind Supply Digital
```

**Follow-up 1 (value-add):**
```
SUBJECT: re: [Business Name]

Hi [Name],

No pressure — if useful, happy to send a quick rundown of where [Business Name]
currently shows up for the searches that matter most in [City]. Just say the word.

[Rep Name]
```

**Follow-up 2:**
```
SUBJECT: Still worth a look?

Hi [Name],

Circling back — totally get it if this isn't a priority right now. If timing
changes, happy to reconnect.

[Rep Name]
```

**Breakup email:**
```
SUBJECT: Closing the loop

Hi [Name],

Haven't heard back, so I'll assume now isn't the right time — no worries at all.
I'm easy to reach if that changes.

[Rep Name]
```

> **⚠️ Compliance Note:** Commercial email is subject to CAN-SPAM (clear opt-out mechanism, honor unsubscribes promptly, accurate sender identification). Confirm the sending tool/domain setup meets these requirements before scaling volume.

## Generic SMS Templates

```
SMS 1: "Hi [Name], [Rep] here from Kind Supply Digital — left you a voicemail
about [Business Name]'s website/Google visibility. Worth 10 min this week?"

SMS 2 (follow-up): "Following up, [Name] — happy to send a quick note on what
we're seeing for [Business Name] if a call's not easy right now."
```

> **⚠️ Compliance Note:** SMS outreach is regulated by the TCPA. Confirm with Marcel that the outreach approach (e.g., manual 1:1 texts to publicly listed business numbers vs. any automated/bulk texting) fits current compliance guidance before scaling volume. Always honor "STOP" requests immediately.

## Generic LinkedIn Templates

```
Connection request: "Hi [Name] — came across [Business Name], would love to
connect."

Follow-up message (after accepted): "Thanks for connecting! Quick question —
how's [Business Name] doing for direct website/search inquiries these days?
Curious if it's worth a quick conversation."
```

Keep LinkedIn messages short and low-pressure — it's a lower-intensity channel than call/text, better suited to warming someone up before a call than closing an appointment cold.

---

## Generic Voicemail Template

```
"Hi [Name], it's [Rep] with Kind Supply Digital — quick question about how
[Business Name] is showing up online and generating new business right now.
I'll follow up with a text/email so it's easy to reconnect. Talk soon."
```

Rules: never pitch on voicemail. Always follow within minutes with a text or email so the voicemail has a clear next action attached to it.
