# Niche Playbook: Law Firms

Covers: personal injury (PI), immigration, estate planning, family law, DUI/criminal defense, and similar practice areas.

---

## 1. Niche Snapshot

Law firms range from solo practitioners to small partnerships. Many practice areas here are intensely competitive in paid search (PI especially), which makes organic visibility and a credible, conversion-focused site disproportionately valuable. Attorneys tend to be skeptical of marketing pitches — they've usually been cold-called by SEO companies and legal-directory sales reps before. Credibility and specificity land better than energy.

---

## 2. Why They Need Us (LEAK Mapped to This Niche)

| LEAK Letter | Common Pattern in Law Firms |
|---|---|
| **L — Look** | Site looks like a template, doesn't differentiate the firm from every other firm in the practice area |
| **E — Experience** | No clear intake path (case evaluation form, click-to-call), especially bad on mobile where most PI/DUI searches happen post-incident |
| **A — Appear** | Outranked by legal directories (Avvo, FindLaw-style listings) and larger firms with bigger ad budgets |
| **K — Keep-up** | Reliant on referral network or slow, inconsistent inbound; case volume inconsistent month to month |

---

## 3. Decision-Maker Map

| Title | Notes |
|---|---|
| Partner / Managing Attorney | Primary decision-maker at small-to-mid firms |
| Owner (solo practice) | Direct decision-maker, often also the person answering their own calls between court appearances |

**Gatekeepers:** paralegals or office managers screen calls often. Attorneys are hard to reach live during business hours — early morning, lunch, or end-of-day calls tend to land better.

---

## 4. Tone & Compliance Notes

- Attorney advertising is regulated by state bar rules (e.g., restrictions on guarantees, required disclaimers, rules around testimonials). This is the firm's responsibility to manage with their own bar counsel — but reps should never promise specific case outcomes, guaranteed case volume, or "guaranteed results" of any kind. Speak in terms of visibility, inquiries, and process — never guaranteed wins or guaranteed lead counts.
- Avoid language that could read as "ambulance chasing," especially in PI conversations — position the work as improving how the firm presents itself and gets found, not as manufacturing cases.

## 5. Cold Call Script (Niche Version)

```
STEP 1 — OPENER
"Hey [Name], this is [Rep] — I know this is a cold call, got 30 seconds?"

STEP 2 — REASON FOR CALL
"I was looking at [Firm Name]'s website and had a quick question about how new
cases are finding you right now — got a second?"

STEP 3 — PERMISSION
"Mind if I ask a couple quick questions?"

STEP 4 — LEAK DISCOVERY
"When's the last time the site itself was updated?"
"If someone searches '[practice area] attorney [city]' right now, does the firm
show up, or mostly the big directories and bigger firms?"
"How many new case inquiries come directly from the website each month right now?"

STEP 5 — VALUE BRIDGE
"That tracks with a lot of what we see in [practice area] — a lot of strong firms
are getting buried under directory listings and bigger ad budgets even though the
firm itself is a better fit for the client."

STEP 6 — ASK FOR APPOINTMENT
"Worth 20 minutes to actually look at where things stand? I've got [time] or
[time], which's better?"
```

## 10. Common Objections (Niche-Specific) — P.V.R.A.

### "We don't want to look like we're chasing ambulances"
> **V:** "Completely understand — that's a real reputational line for a firm."
> **R:** "This isn't about being aggressive, it's about making sure people who are already searching for a [practice area] attorney can actually find and trust your firm instead of a directory ad."
> **A:** "Worth seeing what that looks like without changing your firm's tone at all?"

### "We're all referral-based, don't need this"
> **V:** "That's a strong position to be in, honestly."
> **R:** "A lot of firms we work with feel the same way — this usually isn't a replacement for referrals, more of a hedge so case volume doesn't swing as hard month to month."
> **A:** "Worth a quick look at what a direct pipeline on top of referrals could add?"

### "I need to loop in my partner"
> **V:** "Makes sense, no problem."
> **R:** (none needed)
> **A:** "Would it help if I sent over a short summary you could forward, or would a quick 3-way call work better?"

## 11. Good vs. Bad Example

**Scenario:** Attorney says "we don't do digital marketing, all referral-based."

| ❌ Bad Rep | ✅ Good Rep |
|---|---|
| "Well everyone needs a good website these days!" (dismisses their point, generic) | "That's a strong position — a lot of firms we talk to are the same way. Out of curiosity, if referrals slowed down for a stretch, is there a backup plan, or would that actually hurt?" (validates, then probes real exposure) |
