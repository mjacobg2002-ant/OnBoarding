# Niche Playbook: Behavioral Health

Covers: mental health treatment, substance abuse treatment, assisted living, IOP/PHP/RTC programs, therapy groups.

---

## 1. Niche Snapshot

These are businesses where trust, discretion, and credibility matter enormously — someone searching for treatment (or a family member searching on their behalf) is often in crisis or highly stressed. A dated, confusing, or untrustworthy-looking website doesn't just lose a lead — it can actively turn away someone who needs help *right now*. That framing is genuinely useful in conversation, not just a sales angle.

Many of these operators are clinicians first, business operators second. They may be less digitally sophisticated than a law firm or home services company, and often got their current site built quickly during initial licensing/launch and haven't touched it since.

---

## 2. Why They Need Us (LEAK Mapped to This Niche)

| LEAK Letter | Common Pattern in Behavioral Health |
|---|---|
| **L — Look** | Site built years ago at launch, generic stock photography, doesn't reflect the quality of clinical staff/facility |
| **E — Experience** | No clear "verify insurance" or "admissions" call-to-action; forms that feel clinical/cold instead of reassuring |
| **A — Appear** | Heavy competition from large aggregator sites and paid treatment-referral marketplaces pushing independent facilities down the page |
| **K — Keep-up** | Heavy reliance on referral sources (hospitals, therapists, word of mouth) with little direct inbound from search |

---

## 3. Decision-Maker Map

| Title | Notes |
|---|---|
| Owner / Founder | Often a clinician (LCSW, psychiatrist, addiction specialist) who founded the facility |
| Clinical Director | Sometimes the operational decision-maker, especially in larger programs |
| Practice Owner (therapy groups) | Direct decision-maker in smaller group practices |

**Gatekeepers:** front desk/admissions coordinators are common. Be respectful of the sensitivity of what they do all day (fielding calls from people in crisis) — don't be pushy on the phone with them specifically.

---

## 4. Tone & Compliance Notes

- Never claim specific clinical outcomes or imply KSD has clinical expertise — we build websites and marketing, we are not a clinical authority.
- Intake/contact forms in this space often touch sensitive personal information. It's fair to position that KSD builds forms and workflows **with privacy in mind** — do **not** claim the company is "HIPAA compliant" as a blanket statement unless Marcel has explicitly confirmed that language with legal counsel.
- Lead with respect for how sensitive this category is. Avoid casual or salesy language when referencing patients/clients in crisis.

> **🔧 Confirm with Marcel:** exact approved language around privacy/compliance claims before reps use it in writing (email/SMS) rather than just live conversation.

## 5. Cold Call Script (Niche Version)

```
STEP 1 — OPENER
"Hey [Name], this is [Rep] — I know this is a cold call, got 30 seconds?"

STEP 2 — REASON FOR CALL
"I was actually looking at [Facility Name]'s website and had a quick question about
how new clients or families are finding you online — got a second?"

STEP 3 — PERMISSION
"Mind if I ask a couple quick questions?"

STEP 4 — LEAK DISCOVERY
"When's the last time the website was updated?"
"If a family is searching for [service, e.g., 'IOP program near me'] right now, does
[Facility Name] show up, or mostly the big referral sites?"
"How much of your intake volume comes from the website directly versus
referrals/word of mouth?"

STEP 5 — VALUE BRIDGE
"That's really common in this space — a lot of great programs are getting buried
under the big treatment-marketplace sites even though the site itself doesn't
reflect the quality of care you're actually providing."

STEP 6 — ASK FOR APPOINTMENT
"I'd love to grab 20 minutes to look at this properly — I've got [time] or [time],
which works better?"
```

## 10. Common Objections (Niche-Specific) — P.V.R.A.

### "We're a clinical practice, not really a marketing-focused business"
> **V:** "Totally fair — most of the best programs are clinician-led first."
> **R:** "That's actually exactly the gap — a lot of the best clinical programs have the weakest online presence because marketing isn't the focus, which means families searching for help find the flashier program instead of the better one."
> **A:** "Worth 15 minutes to see where that gap actually is for you?"

### "How do you handle privacy/HIPAA on the website?"
> **V:** "Great question, glad you're thinking about that."
> **R:** "We build every form and workflow with privacy top of mind — happy to walk through exactly how that works on a call rather than get into specifics over the phone."
> **A:** "Want to grab time to go through it properly?"

### "We already get enough referrals, don't need more marketing"
> **V:** "Good position to be in."
> **R:** "Out of curiosity — if a referral source dried up tomorrow, how exposed would that leave you? A lot of programs we talk to want a direct pipeline as a hedge, not a replacement."
> **A:** "Worth a quick look at what that could add on top of referrals?"

## 11. Good vs. Bad Example

**Scenario:** Owner says "we tried a marketing agency last year, it was a waste of money."

| ❌ Bad Rep | ✅ Good Rep |
|---|---|
| "Oh well we're totally different, we actually get results." (defensive, generic, no curiosity) | "That's frustrating — can I ask what specifically didn't work? Was it communication, results, or just not built for a program like yours?" (Pause → Validate → genuine question) |
