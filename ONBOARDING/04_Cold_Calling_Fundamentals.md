# Cold Calling Fundamentals
### The 6-Step Cold Call Flow

---

## Operating Principles (Not Motivation — Just Standards)

- Every dial has one job: earn the next 30 seconds of attention. Not close the deal. Not explain the whole company. Just earn 30 more seconds.
- You are calling business owners who are busy and have heard a pitch before. Sound like a person, not a script.
- Qualify before you pitch. Never pitch before you know if there's a real problem worth solving.
- You are never authorized to quote final pricing on a cold call or discovery call — that comes after Marcel sets direction. Full process: `02_Company_Offer_Reference.md`, Section 5.

---

## The 6-Step Cold Call Flow

| Step | Name | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pattern-Interrupt Opener | Avoid sounding like every other cold call. Get a real reaction, not an autopilot brush-off. |
| 2 | Reason For The Call | Give a specific, credible reason you're calling *this* business, not a generic pitch. |
| 3 | Permission / Qualifying Transition | Earn the right to ask a couple of questions. |
| 4 | LEAK Discovery | 2–3 sharp questions that surface real pain (see `03_ICP_and_Qualifying_Framework.md`). |
| 5 | Value Bridge | One sentence connecting what you just heard to an outcome they'd want. Not a pitch. A bridge. |
| 6 | Ask For The Appointment | A direct, assumptive ask for a specific day/time — not "would you be open to a call sometime?" |

### Generic Fill-In-The-Blank Template

```
STEP 1 — OPENER
"Hey [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] — I know this is a cold call, do you have 30 seconds?"

STEP 2 — REASON FOR CALL
"I was actually just looking at [BUSINESS NAME]'s website / Google listing and had a
question about how you're currently generating new [patients/clients/calls] — got a
quick second?"

STEP 3 — PERMISSION / QUALIFYING TRANSITION
"Cool — mind if I ask you two quick questions so I'm not wasting your time?"

STEP 4 — LEAK DISCOVERY (pick 2–3 based on what you saw in research)
"When's the last time the website itself was updated?"
"Roughly how many new [patients/clients/calls] are coming in from the website or
Google each month right now?"
"Are you actively running any ads today, or is it mostly referral/word of mouth?"

STEP 5 — VALUE BRIDGE
"Got it — that actually lines up with a lot of what we see with [NICHE] businesses:
[ONE-SENTENCE OBSERVATION TIED TO WHAT THEY JUST SAID]."

STEP 6 — ASK FOR THE APPOINTMENT
"I'd love to grab 20 minutes to actually dig into this properly — I've got [DAY/TIME]
or [DAY/TIME], which works better?"
```

---

## Tonality & Pacing

- Slow down on your opener. Most reps rush the first line because they're nervous — that's exactly what makes it sound like a script.
- Use a downward inflection when asking for the appointment (statement-like), not an upward, uncertain one.
- Silence after you ask a question is fine. Do not fill it.
- Mirror their pace — if they're clipped and fast, don't ramble; if they're relaxed, don't rush them.

---

## Voicemail Philosophy

Never leave a full pitch on voicemail. The job of a voicemail is to make the follow-up text/email make sense, not to sell.

```
"Hey [NAME], it's [YOUR NAME] with Kind Supply Digital — I had a quick question about
[BUSINESS NAME]'s website and how you're generating new [patients/clients/calls] right
now. I'll shoot you a text/email right after this so it's easy to get back to me. Talk soon."
```

Always follow a voicemail with a text or email within minutes — same day, same touch. See `11_Outreach_Templates_Multichannel.md` for the full cadence.

---

## Gatekeeper Navigation

Front desk staff, office managers, and receptionists are common in all three niches. A few rules:

- Be direct and pleasant, not cagey. Cagey reads as a scam call.
- Give a real, short reason: *"I'm following up on something with [NAME] about the website — is he/she in today?"*
- If pushed for details you're not ready to give a stranger at the front desk, it's fine to say: *"It's a quick business question for the owner directly — best time to catch them?"*
- If blocked outright, ask for the best time to try again, or the owner's direct line/email. Don't argue with the gatekeeper — they're not the decision-maker and won't be won over by pressure.
- Log what you learn (name of gatekeeper, best callback window) in HubSpot so the next touch is smarter, not a cold repeat.

## Good vs. Bad: Openers

| ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
|---|---|
| "Hi, how are you today? Do you have a few minutes to talk about your marketing?" | "Hey [Name], I know this is a cold call — got 30 seconds?" |
| "This is [Rep] calling from Kind Supply Digital, we help businesses like yours grow online..." (full pitch, no pause) | "I was looking at your Google listing and had a quick question about how new [patients/clients] are finding you — got a second?" |
| Apologizing for calling ("Sorry to bother you...") | Owning the call directly and briefly |
